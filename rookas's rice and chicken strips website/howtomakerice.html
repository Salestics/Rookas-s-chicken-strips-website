How to make rice

Making rice is a simple process that can be done in a few easy steps. Here is how you can make perfect rice every time:

    Before Starting:
  1. Choose Your Rice: There are many types of rice available, such as white rice, brown rice, jasmine rice, basmati rice, and more. Choose the type of rice that you prefer or that suits your recipe.
  2. How Will You Cook It?: people commonly use a pot with a lid to cook rice on the stovetop, or a rice cooker. Today I will tell you how to make rice with a rice cooker and a pot

Cooking Rice with a pot:

  1. Measure the Rice: Start by measuring the amount of rice you want to cook. A common ratio is 1 cup of rice to 2 cups of water, but this can vary depending on the type of rice you are using.
  2. Rinse the Rice: Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This helps to remove excess starch and prevents the rice from becoming too sticky.
  3. Boil the Water: In a pot, bring the measured water to a boil. You can add a pinch of salt for flavor if desired.
  4. Add the Rice: Once the water is boiling, add the rinsed rice to the pot. Stir it briefly to prevent sticking.
  5. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a lid, and let it simmer for about 18-20 minutes for white rice (or longer for brown rice). Avoid lifting the lid during this time, as it can release steam and affect the cooking process.
  6. Fluff and Serve: After the cooking time is up, turn off the heat and let the rice sit covered for an additional 5 minutes. Then, fluff the rice with a fork before serving.

Cooking Rice with a Rice Cooker:

  1. Measure the Rice: Start by measuring the amount of rice you want to cook. A common ratio is 1 cup of rice to 2 cups of water, but this can vary depending on the type of rice you are using.
  2. Rinse the Rice: Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Wash the rice with either your hands, the rice cooker spoon, or another utensil for at least 4-10 times. This helps to remove excess starch and prevents the rice from becoming too sticky.
  3. Add Rice and Water to the Cooker: Place the rinsed rice in the rice cooker and add the appropriate amount of water based on the type of rice you are using.
  4. Start the Cooker: Close the lid of the rice cooker and turn it on. The rice cooker will automatically cook the rice and switch to a "keep warm" mode once it's done.
  5. Fluff and Serve: Once the rice cooker indicates that the rice is done, let it sit for a few minutes before opening the lid. Then, fluff the rice with a fork before serving.

Enjoy your perfectly cooked rice as a side dish or as a base for your favorite meals!

More Variants

Just making normal rice is can be boring at times. There are many dishes that you can make with rice!

You can make:

  • rice (vegetable, chicken, shrimp)

  • Risotto (mushroom, parmesan, seafood)

  • Paella (seafood, mixed)

  • Rice pilaf

  • Sushi (maki, nigiri)

  • Onigiri (rice balls)

  • Rice porridge/congee (savory, sweet)

  • Rice pudding (sweet)

  • Arancini (fried rice balls)

  • Bibimbap

  • Rice and beans

  • Jambalaya

  • Biryani (chicken, lamb, vegetable)

  • Nasi goreng (Indonesian fried rice)
