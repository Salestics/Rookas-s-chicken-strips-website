What are chicken strips?

Chicken strips, also known as chicken tenders, chicken fingers, or chicken goujons, are a popular dish made from boneless, skinless chicken breast meat that is cut into strips. These strips are typically breaded and fried until they are crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. Chicken strips are often served with various dipping sauces and are a favorite among both children and adults.

Fun facts about chicken strips:

  • Chicken strips are believed to have originated in the United States, with various claims to their invention. Some say they were first made in the 1970s, while others trace their roots back to earlier decades.
  • Chicken strips are often made using chicken breast meat, but they can also be made from other parts of the chicken, such as thighs or wings.
  • There are many variations of chicken strips around the world, with different seasonings and breading styles. For example, in some regions, chicken strips may be coated in a spicy batter or seasoned with unique herbs and spices.
  • Chicken strips are a popular menu item at fast-food restaurants and are often served as a quick and convenient meal option.
  • In addition to being fried, chicken strips can also be baked or grilled for a healthier alternative.

Whether you enjoy them as a snack, appetizer, or main course, chicken strips are a delicious and versatile dish that has become a staple in many cuisines around the world!
